How the Art Piece spoke out to me

What it looked like

This painting stood out to me the most because, first, it looked beautiful, and second, it spoke to me. The painting was made by the artist who is named Woody Gwyn, and theis painting is titled Manzano/Primavera.


What it meant to me

Now for the important part and the whole reason for making this website in the first place: what it meant to me. At first glance, anyone would look at it for a moment and mostly think that it is just pretty—that’s what I did at first too. But then, after a while, I started to think about it: what does it mean to me? And then that’s when I noticed it. On the top right corner of the canvas, I noticed that the sky was visible, but on the top left corner, it had no sky and was blocked by the flowers. As I looked at the bottom of both sides, I also noticed that on the left side, it was beautiful, and the grass was as green as it could be. However, on the right side, I saw that the grass was green at first but then became drier afterwards. So to me, I felt like it was portraying freedom. The right side of the painting was the freedom side since the sky was visible but just barely, whereas the left side was the side where there was no freedom. I came to this conclusion because, to me, I felt like the left side was showing how the road is pretty but you won't gain freedom if you stay in that comfort zone. On the right side, the path looks pretty good at first but then gets harder as you progress increasingly into freedom, or the sky. If you stay in your comfort zone, it may look good, but you will never truly have access to that freedom. On the right side, if you take the path that doesn’t look pretty but if you keep going, then that freedom will soon be yours. No matter how the path looks in the end, it will all be worth it when you have truly gained freedom.
